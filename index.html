1. Essential Components

  • Water Tank: The reservoir for water.
  • Water Filling Opening: Port used to add water.
  • Steam Button: Controls Steam Boost and Continuous Steam functions.
  • Thermostat Knob: Rotary dial for adjusting temperature.
  • Power Indicator Light: On while heating; turns OFF when the desired temperature is reached.
  • Steam Regulator: Slider to set steam quantity (0, 1, ECO, 3, Self-Clean).
  • Soleplate: The heated, non-stick contact surface.
  • Anti-Calc Filter: Internal element to minimize scale build-up.

2. Quick Start and Preparation

Water Warning: Use Demineralized or Distilled Water Only.

1

Fill and Prepare

Ensure the iron is unplugged. Set the Thermostat Knob to MIN. Fill the tank with distilled water through the Water Filling Opening, making sure not to exceed the MAX level indicator.

2

Set and Connect

Select the temperature based on your fabric using the Thermostat Knob (see guide below). Connect the appliance to the power mains. The Power Indicator Light will illuminate.

3

Ready Signal

Wait for the iron to heat up. The iron is ready when the Power Indicator Light turns OFF (typically within 60 seconds). Begin ironing only after the light has extinguished.

4. Self-Cleaning & Maintenance

Self-Cleaning Procedure (Monthly)

  1. Fill the tank 3/4 full with distilled water.
  2. Plug In and set Thermostat to MAX.
  3. Wait until the light turns OFF, indicating full heat.
  4. Unplug the iron for safety.
  5. Crucial Step: Set the Steam Regulator to SELF-CLEAN.
  6. Hold the iron horizontally over a sink. Hot water and steam will flush out mineral deposits.
  7. Once the tank is empty, allow the iron to cool completely on its heel before putting it away.

After Every Use

Set Thermostat to MIN and Unplug from the mains.

Empty the remaining water from the water tank completely to prevent scale overnight.

‚ùì 5. Troubleshooting FAQs

Why is my iron leaking water?

A1 (Check Setting): Ensure the Steam Regulator is not accidentally set to the SELF-CLEAN position.

A2 (Check Temperature): The soleplate is likely not hot enough. Steam requires a minimum of the ¬∑¬∑ setting. If it's too cool, the water will leak as condensation rather than vaporizing.

Why is no steam coming out?

A1 (Heat Level): Steam is disabled on the MIN or ¬∑ settings. Set the temperature to ¬∑¬∑ or higher.

A2 (Wait Time): Did you wait until the Power Indicator Light turned OFF? If the light is still on, the iron is not ready.

A3 (Regulator Position): Confirm the Steam Regulator is set to 1, ECO, or 3, not the "0" (Dry Ironing) setting.

6. Critical Safety Precautions

  • Always unplug the iron before any water filling, emptying, or maintenance.
  • Risk of Burns: Do not touch the hot soleplate. Always place the iron on a stable, heat-resistant surface when resting.
  • Keep the iron and its power cord out of reach of children at all times.
  • Never operate the iron if the cord or appliance casing is damaged.
